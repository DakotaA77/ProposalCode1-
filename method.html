Methods

I’ve chose the qualitative method of one-on-one interviewing of women and nonbinary students at PSU. I chose this method for a few reasons:

  • Personal interviews allow for better data on specific impacts of gendered language that you might not be able to find in other databases
  • Participants will be able to self describe their gender instead of being categorized in a way that could be inaccurate (by sex like on the PSU By Numbers Page
  • My identity as a nonbinary student allows me access to first hand experience and relationships with other nonbinary students at PSU
  • First hand experience is valuable and can be coded to see if there is an overlap or pattern in specific impacts

    • Participants: Women and nonbinary students at Portland State University

  • PSU is an academic space that uses gendered language that could impact or discourage students
  • The focus is on students, specifically women and nonbinary students, who are disportionatly impacted by gendered language

    • Participants I Would Exclude:

  • People who don’t go to PSU as to keep the sample concise and specific
  • PSU students who don’t identify as being nonbinary or women because their experience doesn’t pertain to the study questions
  • PSU faculty because they are not students
